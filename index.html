The B2B Visibility Problem, in One Paragraph

Polyform has operated in Salem, Oregon since 1982 and has the capabilities to serve buyers across the entire Pacific Northwest — agriculture, industrial, construction, and consumer goods. But when a procurement manager at a Portland-area construction firm searches "PVC extrusion manufacturer Oregon," or a farm supply company in the Willamette Valley looks for "custom grow tube plastic extrusion," Polyform does not appear in the top results. The Semrush listing scan confirmed Bing Places is completely absent — a platform that powers Microsoft Advertising, Windows local search, and voice assistants for millions of B2B buyers — and Facebook has a name inconsistency ("Polyform Inc." with a period) that fragments NAP signals across the web. Competitors with half the industry experience but twice the digital investment capture every one of those leads. With 0.9% of the total organic traffic in its competitive set and 8 of 25 scanned directories either broken or missing entirely, Polyform is invisible at the exact moment a buyer is ready to make a decision.
